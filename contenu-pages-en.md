## OUR WAGYU PAGE

### Title: Our Wagyu

**Subtitle:** Understanding what makes Wagyu genuinely different.

---

### What Is Wagyu?

"Wagyu" (和牛) is a Japanese term meaning literally "Japanese cow" — *wa* (Japanese) and *gyu* (cow). It refers to four specific Japanese cattle breeds, the most famous being Kuroge Washu (Black Wagyu), the source of the legendary Kobe beef.

What sets Wagyu apart from every other breed is genetic: these animals have an extraordinary ability to develop intramuscular fat — what you see as **marbling** in the meat. This fine network of fat, distributed evenly throughout the muscle, melts at low temperatures and gives the beef a tenderness, a richness, and a depth of flavor that simply has no equal.

---

### Marbling: The Science Behind the Taste

Marbling quality is measured using the **BMS** (Beef Marble Score) scale, ranging from 1 (minimal marbling, comparable to standard beef) to 12 (extreme marbling, Kobe Grade A5).

| BMS | Description | Comparison |
|-----|-------------|------------|
| 1-2 | Standard | Regular beef |
| 3-4 | Superior | Canadian AAA |
| 5-6 | Excellent | Typical Wagyu F1 |
| 7-9 | Exceptional | Fullblood Wagyu |
| 10-12 | Legendary | Kobe Grade A5 |

The Wagyu F1 Cross we carry typically scores between **BMS 4 and BMS 7** depending on the cut — well beyond even Canada's finest AAA beef, and at a fraction of the price of Fullblood Wagyu.

---

### F1 Cross: The Best Value Wagyu Experience

**Wagyu F1 Cross** (first-generation crossbred Wagyu) is the offspring of a purebred Wagyu sire and a Black Angus dam. The combination is deliberate and effective:

- **Wagyu genetics** bring the marbling, the tenderness, and the healthy mono-unsaturated fatty acids (omega-9)
- **Angus genetics** bring a fuller muscle structure, strong yield, and feeding efficiency

The result: remarkably marbled beef with generous cut sizes, at a price that actually makes sense for families. It's the choice of countless top chefs who want to deliver a true Wagyu experience without locking their menus into stratospheric price points.

---

### From Brazilian Farm to Your Quebec Table

Our Wagyu F1 Cross beef comes from partner farms in Brazil selected on three criteria:

1. **Genetic quality**: herds from documented Japanese Wagyu bloodlines
2. **Farming standards**: no growth hormones, supervised plant-based feed, strong animal welfare protocols
3. **FAMBRAS Halal Certification**: supervised Halal slaughter, full traceability documentation, unbroken chain of custody

The beef arrives in Quebec and is processed by our team in certified facilities. Every cut is labeled, vacuum-sealed, and identified with full traceability information.

---

### Why Wagyu Changes How You Think About Beef

Eating Wagyu is understanding that beef can be something more. The texture melts rather than chews. The flavor is long, complex, subtly sweet — a result of a fat profile closer to olive oil than conventional beef. You eat less, but you savor more. Nutritionists also note that Wagyu fat is predominantly unsaturated, making it a more heart-friendly choice than regular beef.

At Lassonde, we say it all the time: try it once, and you'll understand everything.

## HALAL CERTIFICATION PAGE

### Title: Halal Certification

**Subtitle:** Our commitment to Muslim families in Quebec and beyond.

---

### What Is FAMBRAS Certification?

**FAMBRAS** — *Federação Árabe Brasileira de Alimentação* — is Brazil's Halal certification body, founded under the Federation of Arab-Islamic Entities of Brazil. It is recognized and accredited by:

- **JAKIM** (Malaysia), the world's leading Halal certification authority
- **MUI** (Indonesia), the world's largest Islamic organization
- Halal authorities of the UAE, Saudi Arabia, and over 30 countries
- The **Canadian Council of Muslim Organizations** and Quebec Islamic associations

FAMBRAS certification covers the entire process: farming, slaughter, processing, packaging, and transport. It isn't a label — it's a continuous, audited system.

---

### The Halal Slaughter Process

Halal slaughter follows these requirements, supervised at every step by a certified religious inspector:

1. **The animal is alive and healthy** at the time of slaughter
2. **The slaughterman is a trained Muslim** who recites the *Basmala* (بسم الله) before each act
3. **The cut is swift and precise** using a sharp instrument, minimizing suffering
4. **Blood is fully drained** through the slaughter wound — an essential requirement for Halal compliance
5. **No cross-contamination** with Haram products is permitted anywhere in the production chain

Each step is documented, filmed where applicable, and verifiable through the FAMBRAS traceability program.

---

### What This Means for You

When you buy from Les Élevages Lassonde, you can be certain that:

- **The beef was slaughtered Halal at the source**, in Brazil, before any shipping
- **The chain of custody is unbroken** from the slaughterhouse to your door
- **Processing in Quebec** takes place in a facility that maintains Halal separation protocols
- **Every package** carries a traceable lot number linked to the original farm
- **Certification documents** are available upon request

---

### Why This Matters for Muslim Families

For Muslim families, the question "is this really Halal?" is not trivial. Halal certification exists precisely to answer that question with evidence, not just assurance.

In Quebec, the supply of premium Halal-certified beef is genuinely limited. Finding high-end cuts — Wagyu, Tomahawk, Ribeye — that are also certified by an internationally recognized body has been, until now, nearly impossible. Les Élevages Lassonde fills that gap directly.

## COOKING & RECIPES PAGE

### Title: Cooking & Recipes

**Subtitle:** Getting the most from your Wagyu, at home.

---

### Cooking Fundamentals

Wagyu behaves differently than conventional beef. Its intramuscular fat melts at lower temperatures, meaning heat moves through the meat differently. Here are the rules that matter.

**Recommended Internal Temperatures:**

| Doneness | Internal Temperature |
|----------|---------------------|
| Rare | 49–52°C (120–125°F) |
| Medium-Rare ✓ Recommended | 54–57°C (130–135°F) |
| Medium | 60–63°C (140–145°F) |
| Well Done | Not recommended for Wagyu |

**Note:** Wagyu is at its best medium-rare. Beyond that, the intramuscular fat fully renders and the unique texture is lost.

**Essential Tools:**
- Instant-read meat thermometer (non-negotiable)
- Cast iron or heavy stainless steel pan
- Tongs — never pierce the meat with a fork

**Resting the Meat:**
Let your cut rest 5 to 10 minutes after cooking, loosely tented with foil. The juices redistribute and the temperature equalizes.

**Bringing to Room Temperature:**
Always remove Wagyu from the refrigerator 30 to 45 minutes before cooking. Cold center + hot surface = uneven cook.

---

### Recipe 1: Pan-Seared Filet Mignon with Brown Butter

**Prep time:** 5 min | **Cook time:** 8–10 min | **Serves:** 2

**Ingredients:**
- 2 Wagyu Filet Mignons (180–220g each) from Lassonde
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter *(see Halal-strict variation below)*
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed (skin on)
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- Fleur de sel, freshly cracked black pepper
- 1 tbsp grapeseed or avocado oil (high smoke point)

**Method:**
1. Rest fillets at room temperature for 40 minutes. Pat completely dry with paper towel.
2. Season generously with salt on both sides just before cooking.
3. Heat cast iron pan over high heat with the oil until shimmering (nearly smoking).
4. Place fillets in pan. Do not move them for 2 minutes. Flip. 2 minutes on the other side.
5. Reduce to medium-low. Add butter, garlic, and thyme.
6. Continuously baste the fillets with the melted butter for 2–3 minutes.
7. Check with thermometer: 54°C for medium-rare.
8. Rest for 5 minutes. Finish with fleur de sel.

**Halal-Strict Variation (no butter):**
Replace butter with **3 tbsp extra-virgin coconut oil** or a **high-quality olive oil**. Continuously basting with garlic- and thyme-infused oil produces an excellent result — slightly different, equally delicious.

---

### Recipe 2: Tomahawk Wagyu BBQ — Reverse Sear Method

**Prep time:** 15 min | **Cook time:** 1.5–2 hr | **Serves:** 3–4

The reverse sear is the definitive technique for large cuts like the Tomahawk. Start low and slow in the oven to cook it through evenly, then finish on a screaming-hot grill for the perfect crust.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 Wagyu Tomahawk from Lassonde (900g–1.2kg typically)
- Olive oil
- 2 tsp coarse salt
- 1 tsp coarsely ground black pepper
- 1 tsp garlic powder (optional)
- Smoking wood (if using BBQ smoker): oak, hickory, or applewood

**Method:**

*Step 1 — Prep (ideally the night before):*
Season the Tomahawk with salt on all sides. Place on a wire rack over a baking sheet and refrigerate uncovered for 12–24 hours. This step dries the surface for optimal crust formation.

*Step 2 — Low-temperature oven cook:*
1. Preheat oven to 120°C (250°F).
2. Place Tomahawk on rack and sheet. Insert thermometer probe into the thickest part of the meat.
3. Cook until internal temperature reaches 48°C (approximately 60–90 minutes depending on thickness).
4. Remove and rest uncovered for 10 minutes.

*Step 3 — Final sear:*
1. Preheat your BBQ to maximum heat (or cast iron pan over very high heat).
2. Brush lightly with olive oil.
3. Sear 1–2 minutes per side and on the edges — you're looking for a deep, dark-golden crust.
4. Target final temperature: 54–57°C (medium-rare).

*Step 4 — Service:*
Rest for 10 minutes. Slice against the bone, perpendicular to the grain. Finish with fleur de sel.

---

### Recipe 3: Texas-Style Smoked Brisket

**Prep time:** 20 min | **Smoke time:** 12–16 hr | **Serves:** 8–12

Brisket (la poitrine) is the crown jewel of American BBQ. With Wagyu, the natural fat content creates results of extraordinary tenderness and juiciness that standard brisket simply can't match.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 Wagyu Brisket from Lassonde (4–6kg, flat + point)
- **Texas Classic Rub:**
  - 3 tbsp coarse kosher salt
  - 3 tbsp coarsely cracked black pepper
  - 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  - 1 tsp cayenne pepper
- Smoking wood: oak, hickory, or pecan

**Method:**

*The night before:*
1. Trim the brisket — leave approximately 1cm of fat cap on top. Don't remove it all.
2. Apply the rub generously on all surfaces.
3. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate 8–12 hours.

*Smoke day:*
1. Remove brisket from the fridge 1 hour before smoking.
2. Preheat smoker to 107–120°C (225–250°F). Stabilize the temperature.
3. Place brisket fat-cap up.
4. Smoke until internal temp reaches 74°C (the "stall" — temperature will plateau for hours. This is normal. Don't panic, don't raise the heat.)
5. At 74°C, wrap tightly in butcher paper (or aluminum foil).
6. Continue cooking to 93–96°C internal.
7. Rest wrapped in a cooler (no ice) for at least 1 hour, ideally 2–3 hours.

*Service:* Slice against the grain. The meat should be quivering and dripping with juice. Serve with your favorite BBQ sides.

---

### Recipe 4: Wagyu Brochettes with Sumac-Lemon Marinade

**Prep time:** 20 min + 2 hr marinating | **Cook time:** 10–12 min | **Serves:** 4

A recipe built around the flavors of Levantine and North African cuisine — perfect for summer grilling, family gatherings, and Eid celebrations.

**Ingredients:**
- 600g Wagyu beef from Lassonde (brochette cubes or bavette cut into 3cm pieces)
- **Sumac-Lemon Marinade:**
  - 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  - 2 tbsp sumac
  - Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  - 3 garlic cloves, finely minced
  - 1 tsp ground cumin
  - ½ tsp cinnamon
  - ½ tsp sweet paprika
  - Salt and black pepper
  - 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- **For skewers:** bell peppers, red onion, cherry tomatoes

**Method:**
1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a bowl and mix well.
2. Add the Wagyu cubes and toss to coat evenly.
3. Cover and refrigerate for 2 hours minimum (4 hours is ideal — no more than 6 hours, as lemon acidity will begin to denature the protein).
4. Thread onto skewers, alternating Wagyu, peppers, onion, and tomatoes.
5. Grill on a hot BBQ or grill pan over high heat, 2–3 minutes per side.
6. The Wagyu should remain pink in the center.

**To serve:** Accompany with warm pita bread, labneh (strained yogurt), tabbouleh, and lemon wedges. Finish with a drizzle of olive oil and a dusting of fresh sumac.

## RETURN POLICY

### Title: Returns & Satisfaction Policy

**Effective date:** [To be completed]

---

### The Perishable Nature of Our Products

Les Élevages Lassonde products are perishable food items (fresh and frozen meats). Due to the nature of these products and Canadian food safety regulations, **physical returns are not accepted.** This policy is consistent with CFIA (Canadian Food Inspection Agency) standards for food safety.

---

### General No-Refund Policy

Orders received and conforming to their description are generally non-refundable and non-exchangeable. By placing an order, you acknowledge and accept this condition.

---

### Exceptions — When We Step In

We will replace or refund your order in the following cases:

**1. Our Fulfillment Error**
If you receive a different product than what you ordered (wrong cut, wrong weight, wrong quantity).

**2. Product Damaged During Delivery**
If vacuum-seal packaging is perforated upon receipt, if the cold chain has been compromised (product at unsafe temperature), or if the product shows visible signs of deterioration due to shipping conditions.

**3. Product Not Conforming to Halal Standards**
In the extremely unlikely event that a product does not meet documented Halal standards, we will provide a full replacement and conduct an internal investigation.

---

### Claims Procedure

If you believe you qualify for one of the above exceptions:

1. **Photograph** the product and packaging immediately upon receipt
2. **Contact us within 48 hours** of delivery at [orders@elevageslassonde.ca]
3. **Include** your order number and a description of the issue
4. **Attach photos** to your email
5. We will respond within 24–48 business hours with a resolution

Claims submitted more than 48 hours after delivery cannot be processed for product condition issues.

---

### Cancellations

- Before Thursday 10:00 AM cutoff: full cancellation and complete refund
- After cutoff: order is in preparation and cannot be cancelled
